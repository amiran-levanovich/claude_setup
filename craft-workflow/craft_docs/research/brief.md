# Research — Discovery & Brief

Covers investigating a question, gathering and weighing evidence, and synthesizing findings into a report or recommendation. The discipline: a clear question and success criteria before searching, evidence weighed by source quality, and claims traceable to sources.

## Discovery questions (AskUserQuestion, ≤2 per call)
1. **The question** — what decision or output does this research serve? Phrase it as a precise, answerable question, not a topic.
2. **Hypotheses / priors** — what does the user already believe or expect? (so we can confirm or disconfirm, not just confirm).
3. **Scope & boundaries** — timeframe, geography, market, depth; what's explicitly out of scope.
4. **Source expectations** — required source types (academic, market reports, docs, primary data), credibility bar, recency requirements.
5. **Output shape** — a decision memo, a comparison table, a literature/landscape scan, a full report; length and audience.
6. **Success criteria** — what makes this research "good enough to act on"? Seeds the rubric.
