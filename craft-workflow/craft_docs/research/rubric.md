# Research — Acceptance Rubric

The "tests" for a research deliverable. Specialize to the brief in Step 3; run every dimension in the Step 5 review loop.

## 1. Question & scope
- [ ] The deliverable answers **the question in the brief** — directly, not adjacent to it.
- [ ] Scope boundaries (time/geo/depth) were respected; out-of-scope areas stayed out.
- [ ] The stated **hypotheses were actually tested** — including evidence that disconfirms them, not just supports.

## 2. Evidence quality
- [ ] **Source credibility scored** — each key claim's source is weighed (primary > peer-reviewed > reputable secondary > vendor/marketing > anonymous). Weak sources are labeled as such.
- [ ] **Recency** meets the brief's bar; outdated data is flagged.
- [ ] **Triangulation** — material claims are corroborated by more than one independent source; single-source claims are marked.
- [ ] **No fabrication** — every statistic, quote, and finding is real and retrievable. Nothing invented or misattributed. Uncertainty is stated, not smoothed over.

## 3. Traceability
- [ ] **Every claim links to its source** (citation/URL). A reader can verify any assertion.
- [ ] Direct quotes are accurate and attributed; paraphrases don't distort the source.
- [ ] The user's priors vs. the evidence are clearly distinguished from the researcher's inference.

## 4. Reasoning & balance
- [ ] **Counter-arguments addressed** — the strongest opposing evidence/interpretation is presented, not buried.
- [ ] Conclusions follow from the evidence; confidence is calibrated (don't state "proven" on thin data).
- [ ] Gaps and limitations are stated explicitly (what we couldn't determine and why).

## 5. Synthesis & output
- [ ] **Answers up front** — the bottom-line answer/recommendation leads; supporting detail follows (inverted pyramid).
- [ ] Structured for the audience and output shape in the brief (memo / table / report).
- [ ] Each sub-question from the plan is resolved or explicitly left open with a reason.

### Judgment calls
When the evidence is genuinely mixed or a recommendation depends on the user's risk tolerance, present the options and the trade-offs via AskUserQuestion rather than forcing a single answer. Surface low-confidence conclusions as such.
